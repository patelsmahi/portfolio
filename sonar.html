Sonar

Sonar is a conceptual music discovery app designed to simplify the process of finding new songs that match a listener’s taste. The project began with one question: why does discovering new music still feel so overwhelming? Many users rely on algorithms that feel impersonal or inconsistent, making it difficult to find music that genuinely resonates.

To solve this, I created Sonar — an interactive prototype that transforms music discovery into a personal and engaging experience. Inspired by the familiar swipe interaction, users can listen to short song previews and decide whether they like them or not. Songs they enjoy are automatically saved to a playlist that can be imported to their preferred streaming platform.

Through this project, I explored how small interactions and playful design can make digital experiences feel more human, intuitive, and fun.

Role: UI/UX Designer
Duration: 3 Weeks

Process

Research Personas Design Goals Low-Fidelity High-Fidelity

User Research & Insights

Mapped core tasks like browse, search, and queue, then iterated quick wireframes to validate layout and control placement.


Personas

To guide the design of Sonar, I developed a few personas to represent potential users of the app. These personas helped me stay focused on user motivations and frustrations throughout the design process.


Design Goals

From the research insights, I set 3 clear design goals:

  • 1: Make music discovery fun and engaging.
  • 2: Use a familiar interaction pattern (swiping) to reduce learning time.
  • 3: Keep the interface clean and intuitive to avoid clutter.

Low-Fidelity Wireframe

I sketched out low-fidelity wireframe to explore how a swipe-based flow could be applied to music discovery. These wireframe helped me visualize navigation patterns and experiment with where to place key features like song previews and save options.


High‑Fidelity Wireframe

Using the wireframes as a foundation, I built high-fidelity prototypes to bring the interface to life. The focus was on designing a minimal, visually appealing UI that supported fast navigation and music previews.


Outcome

Results

Through this process, I was able to translate a broad concept into a functional mobile app design. Sonar gave me the chance to practice applying the UI/UX workflow, experiment with creative interaction patterns, and strengthen my skills in mobile app usability and interface design.
